# Setting Up Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

**Category:** Password / Account Access
**Audience:** All employees (mandatory for all accounts)
**Estimated time:** 5 minutes

## Before you start

Install an authenticator app on your phone (Microsoft Authenticator or
Google Authenticator — check which one your company uses) before
starting this process.

## Steps

1. Go to the company's MFA enrollment page (linked from the Self-Service
   Portal home screen under "Security Settings").
2. Log in with your existing username and password.
3. Choose **"Add a method"** → **Authenticator App**.
4. Open the authenticator app on your phone, tap "+" → "Scan QR code",
   and scan the code shown on your screen.
5. Enter the 6-digit code the app generates to confirm the pairing.
6. Optionally add a backup method (SMS to a personal number) in case you
   lose your phone.

## Common issues

- **QR code won't scan**: Use "Enter code manually" instead and type the
  key shown beneath the QR code into the app.
- **Codes don't match / "invalid code" error**: Your phone's clock may be
  out of sync — enable automatic date & time in your phone settings.
- **Lost/new phone**: Use your backup method to log in, then re-enroll
  your new device. If you have no backup method, you'll need identity
  verification through the Service Desk.

## When to contact the Service Desk instead

Loss of your only MFA device with no backup method configured — this
requires identity verification before we can reset your MFA enrollment.
