# Troubleshooting Slow Wi-Fi

**Category:** Network Connectivity
**Audience:** All employees
**Estimated time:** 5 minutes

## Steps

1. Run a quick speed check by visiting a speed test site while connected
   to office/home Wi-Fi. Note the download/upload numbers.
2. Restart your Wi-Fi adapter: turn Wi-Fi off and back on from your
   system tray, or toggle Airplane Mode on laptops.
3. Move closer to the access point/router — walls and distance are the
   most common cause of "slow" that's actually "weak signal."
4. If working from home, check how many devices are on the network at
   once (streaming, gaming consoles, smart TVs all compete for bandwidth).
5. Forget and reconnect to the Wi-Fi network: Settings → Network → Wi-Fi
   → Forget This Network, then reconnect and re-enter the password.
6. Restart your router (unplug for 10 seconds) if you manage your own
   home router.

## When to contact the Service Desk instead

If you're in the office and multiple people nearby report the same
issue (likely an access point problem we need to escalate to
Facilities/Network team), or if speeds stay poor after all steps above
on a wired connection too.
